# How the App Works

## Mission Boundary
The clear area on the map is the current legal play area. The dimmed area is outside the mission boundary.

## Location Warning
If your live location leaves the active play area, the app shows a persistent warning notification, tints the map red, and turns app controls red. During real play, return to the legal area immediately.

## Dev Settings
Press **Status** and **Rules** at the same time to open the hidden dev settings tab. Use it to disable the boundary warning, copy map tap coordinates, switch offline mode, or enable no-UI mode for testing. No-UI mode hides the whole interface and transit overlays; exit it by reloading the app or clicking the map 6 times in a row.

## Dropping a Pin
Touch and hold, or click and hold, on the map to set a hiding pin. The pin must be close enough to an approved RTD stop or station for the app to snap to it. The app then builds a 1/4-mile hiding zone around that approved stop or station.

## Saved Pin
The current pin is saved in this browser. If the page reloads, the app restores the saved pin and the matching hiding zone.

## Reset Map
Use **Reset Map** to clear the saved pin and return to the full original mission boundary.

## Live Tracking
The app uses browser location only while the page is visible. Tracking pauses when the page is hidden, minimized, or closed.

# Custom Rules

## Game Format
Two teams play head-to-head over two rounds. The planned setup is 2 players versus 3 players.

Each team gets one hiding round and one seeking round. The team with the longest survival time wins.

## Starting the Game
The game begins at Denver Union Station.

Roles are chosen by casting lots, such as rolling dice. Once roles are set, location sharing is turned on for the seekers and the first hiding team starts its 75-minute hiding window.

## Hiding Window
The hiding team has exactly **75 minutes** to travel and lock in a final hiding location.

The hiding location must be centered on an approved RTD rail platform or an approved bus stop. Once the hiders commit to that location, they use the app to set the 1/4-mile hiding zone.

## Hunt Clock
Each hunt has a **4-hour hard cap**.

If the seekers catch the hiders before the cap, the round ends immediately. If the seekers do not catch them within 4 hours, the hiders receive the full survival time plus any valid unplayed time-bonus cards.

## Round Switch
When a round ends, the game pauses and everyone meets at the capture station platform or capture bus stop.

Decks are reset there. The next hiding team starts its 75-minute hiding window directly from that location. Teams do not return to Union Station between rounds.

## Tracking Rules
Seekers must enable native Google Maps Location Sharing using the "Until you turn this off" option and share it with the hiders.

Hiders do not share their live location. They remain invisible and use the seekers' shared locations to dodge, plan, and reposition.

## Deductions and Questions
Seekers may not use hider location data. They must rely on movement strategy, the transit network, and answers from the card deck.

Hiders must answer valid card questions according to the translated Denver rules listed below.

## Approved Rail Lines
All RTD Light Rail lines are active: D, E, H, L, R, and W.

The approved Commuter Rail lines are B, G, and N.

The A-Line is banned because of its premium airport pricing.

## Approved Bus Routes
Only the listed bus and BRT routes are legal. Any bus route not listed here is out of bounds for game travel.

Approved east-west routes: 15, 15L, 3, 6, 10, 20, 32, 38, and 43.

Approved north-south and cross-town routes: 0, 0L, 31, 40, 51, 24, 73, 76, and 105.

Approved connector and shuttle routes: FREE, FMR, 83D, 83L, 120X, and FF1.

## Hiding Zone
The final hiding zone is a **1/4-mile radius** centered on the chosen final rail platform or approved bus stop.

The app is the boundary reference for the hiding zone. Players should use the map to verify the legal area before settling in.

## Eating and Breaks
The game uses an "Eat When You Want" rule.

Players should pack lunch in daypacks and eat while riding, hiding, or waiting. Meal breaks do not stop the game clock.

## Card Translation
Standard card wording is translated to Denver transit terms.

**"Express / Regional Trains"** means Commuter Rail lines B, G, and N, plus BRT routes 15L, FF1, and 120X.

**"Local / Urban Trains"** means RTD Light Rail and approved local bus routes.

**"Fare Gates"** or **"Tapping Out"** means stepping off the immediate train platform or bus stop area onto a public sidewalk, street, parking lot, or surrounding area.

**"Line Transfers"** means switching from one line to a different line at any station or stop.

**"Stops Away"** means counting stops or stations in the exact order they appear on the active route being ridden.
